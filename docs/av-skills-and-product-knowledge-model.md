# TechSubbies AV Skills and Product Knowledge Model

## Separation of concerns

The engineer profile should separate:

1. Role skills - what the engineer can do.
2. Product awareness and experience - which brands, platforms and technologies they know.
3. Setup profile - tools, testers, documents, insurance, site cards and equipment ownership.
4. Availability profile - location, working radius, calendar, weekends and holidays.

## Product knowledge recommendation

Product knowledge should be managed as a separate tagging layer, not duplicated inside every role profile.

Each product or technology tag should support these experience levels:

- Aware
- Assisted
- Installed
- Configured
- Commissioned
- Programmed
- Troubleshot
- Certified

## Why

A Control System Programmer and an AV Service Engineer may both know Crestron, but their experience is different.

Examples:

- Control System Programmer: Crestron - Programmed / Certified
- Service Engineer: Crestron - Troubleshot
- Basic AV Installer: Crestron - Assisted
- Design Consultant: Crestron - Aware / Specified

This creates more accurate matching than a simple yes/no product checkbox.

## Role profile structure

Each AV role contains:

- Role summary
- Suitable work types
- Must-have skill summary
- Nice-to-have skill summary
- Expected knowledge
- Defined role aspects
- Typical evidence
- Product knowledge tag suggestions
- Skill groups split into must-have and nice-to-have
